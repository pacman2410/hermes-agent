# Sample User Reports

## Report 1: Promotion Readiness

Profile: Senior individual contributor seeking promotion within 6 months.

Career Health Score: 68 - Stable.

Promotion Readiness Score: 61 - Build Evidence First.

Component breakdown:

- Results: 82
- Visibility: 48
- Relationships: 55
- Leadership Signals: 63
- Strategic Thinking: 58
- Influence: 60

Interpretation:

You are producing meaningful work, but your promotion case is not yet visible enough. The issue is not effort. The issue is packaging, stakeholder awareness, and promotion evidence.

Top strengths:

- Strong measurable results.
- Reliable current-role performance.
- Clear interest in next level.

Top blockers:

- Limited visibility outside immediate manager.
- Evidence is not organized into a promotion case.
- Strategic thinking is not explicit in updates.

Next 7 days:

1. Capture 5 measurable accomplishments in the Promotion Evidence Vault.
2. Ask your manager: "What evidence would make a promotion case credible this cycle?"
3. Identify 3 stakeholders who should understand the impact of your current work.

90-day outcome:

Build a credible promotion case supported by results, visibility, and stakeholder alignment.

Human escalation:

Recommended after the evidence vault has at least 10 entries and manager expectations are clear.

## Report 2: New Manager Readiness

Profile: Team lead preparing for first people-manager role.

Career Health Score: 74 - Stable.

New Manager Readiness Score: 57 - Emerging But Inconsistent.

Leadership Readiness Score: 62 - Emerging Manager.

Component breakdown:

- Role Transition: 70
- Delegation: 42
- Difficult Conversations: 46
- Team Management: 58
- Communication: 72
- Prioritization: 61

Interpretation:

You are showing leadership potential, but you are still operating like the strongest individual contributor on the team. Your main risk is taking on too much yourself instead of building team capacity.

Top strengths:

- Clear communication.
- Good understanding of team priorities.
- Strong ownership.

Top blockers:

- Delegation is underdeveloped.
- Difficult conversations are being delayed.
- 1:1 and team operating rhythm need structure.

Next 7 days:

1. Choose one recurring task to delegate with clear outcomes and check-in points.
2. Draft a 1:1 template for direct reports or project contributors.
3. Practice one feedback conversation using behavior, impact, expectation language.

90-day outcome:

Build the operating habits required to lead through others instead of personally carrying the work.

Human escalation:

Recommended if the user has an upcoming difficult conversation or is already managing direct reports.

## Report 3: Career Health

Profile: Mid-career professional feeling stuck and unclear about next move.

Career Health Score: 46 - Unclear.

Component breakdown:

- Career Clarity: 35
- Role Alignment: 50
- Readiness For Next Move: 42
- Skill Development: 55
- Relationships And Influence: 45
- Action Consistency: 48

Interpretation:

You are not stuck because you lack ability. You are stuck because the next move is undefined. Without a target, your effort is scattered and progress is hard to measure.

Top strengths:

- Willingness to develop.
- Some useful current-role experience.
- Enough stability to make a plan.

Top blockers:

- No clear 90-day career outcome.
- Skills are being developed without a target role.
- Weekly actions are inconsistent.

Next 7 days:

1. Choose one primary 90-day outcome.
2. List 3 possible target roles and eliminate 2 using clear criteria.
3. Complete one small career action before the next check-in.

90-day outcome:

Move from career ambiguity to a clear target path with measurable weekly progress.

Human escalation:

Not required yet. Escalate only if the user cannot choose a direction after two guided exercises.
