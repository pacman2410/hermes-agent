# Assessment And Scoring Specification

## Universal Response Scale

Most scored questions use a 1-5 scale:

1. Strongly disagree or not true.
2. Slightly true.
3. Somewhat true.
4. Mostly true.
5. Strongly true.

Normalize each component:

`component_score = ((average_response - 1) / 4) * 100`

Overall score:

`overall_score = sum(component_score * component_weight)`

## Career Health Assessment

Purpose: measure whether the user's career is moving in a clear, intentional, and sustainable direction.

### Components

| Component | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Career Clarity | 20% |
| Role Alignment | 15% |
| Readiness For Next Move | 20% |
| Skill Development | 15% |
| Relationships And Influence | 15% |
| Action Consistency | 15% |

### Questions

Career Clarity:

1. I can clearly describe the career outcome I want in the next 90 days.
2. I know what role, responsibility level, or opportunity I am moving toward.
3. I know why this next move matters to me.
4. I can tell the difference between a good opportunity and a distraction.

Role Alignment:

5. My current role is helping me build toward my next move.
6. My current responsibilities align with the work I want to do next.
7. My strengths are visible in my current role.
8. My current work gives me useful evidence for my next step.

Readiness For Next Move:

9. I have recent accomplishments that support my next move.
10. I can explain my value clearly to a manager, recruiter, or stakeholder.
11. I understand the expectations of the next role or level.
12. I know the gaps I must close to be ready.

Skill Development:

13. I am actively improving the skills required for my next move.
14. I practice new skills in real work situations.
15. I can show evidence that my skills are improving.
16. I get feedback on the skills that matter most.

Relationships And Influence:

17. I have relationships with people who can support my next move.
18. Key stakeholders understand the value of my work.
19. I communicate my impact clearly and appropriately.
20. I am building influence beyond my immediate responsibilities.

Action Consistency:

21. I take career-building actions every week.
22. I track progress against my goals.
23. I follow through on development commitments.
24. I adjust my plan when circumstances change.

### Score Bands

| Score | Band | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-39 | At Risk | The user lacks direction, momentum, or readiness. |
| 40-59 | Unclear | The user has effort but lacks a coherent operating plan. |
| 60-74 | Stable | The user has a base but needs consistency and prioritization. |
| 75-89 | Building Momentum | The user is progressing with clear leverage points. |
| 90-100 | High Momentum | The user has strong clarity, evidence, and action rhythm. |

### Recommendations

- Low Career Clarity: define a single 90-day outcome and decision criteria.
- Low Role Alignment: identify whether to reposition current role or prepare transition.
- Low Readiness: build evidence, close gaps, and practice value narrative.
- Low Skill Development: select one skill sprint and apply it at work.
- Low Relationships: map stakeholders and create weekly visibility action.
- Low Action Consistency: reduce plan to one high-impact action per week.

## Promotion Readiness Assessment

Purpose: measure whether the user has enough evidence, visibility, relationships, leadership signals, strategic thinking, and influence to make a credible promotion case.

### Components

| Component | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Results | 25% |
| Visibility | 20% |
| Relationships | 15% |
| Leadership Signals | 15% |
| Strategic Thinking | 15% |
| Influence | 10% |

### Questions

Results:

1. I have documented measurable accomplishments from the last 6-12 months.
2. My work has clear business impact.
3. I can connect my results to team, department, or company priorities.
4. I consistently perform at or above expectations for my current level.

Visibility:

5. My manager understands the scope and impact of my work.
6. People outside my immediate team know my contributions.
7. I share progress in ways that are useful, not self-promotional.
8. My work is visible to people who influence promotion decisions.

Relationships:

9. I know who influences promotion decisions in my environment.
10. I have credibility with key stakeholders.
11. I receive useful feedback from my manager or sponsors.
12. I have advocates who can speak to my impact.

Leadership Signals:

13. I help others succeed without being asked.
14. I take ownership beyond my assigned tasks.
15. I model good judgment under pressure.
16. I improve team processes, not just my own work.

Strategic Thinking:

17. I understand the business priorities behind my work.
18. I can explain tradeoffs and recommend a path forward.
19. I identify problems before they become urgent.
20. I propose improvements that align with broader goals.

Influence:

21. I can gain support without relying on authority.
22. I adapt communication to different stakeholders.
23. I handle pushback constructively.
24. I can make a clear case for my recommendations.

### Score Bands

| Score | Band | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-49 | Not Ready To Ask | Asking now is likely premature. |
| 50-69 | Build Evidence First | User needs stronger proof or alignment. |
| 70-84 | Prepare Promotion Case | User may be close but needs packaging and conversation prep. |
| 85-100 | Ready For Conversation | User has enough foundation to begin promotion discussion. |

### Improvement Logic

- Low Results: create an impact inventory and quantify outcomes.
- Low Visibility: build a weekly visibility plan.
- Low Relationships: identify manager, sponsor, stakeholder, and peer map.
- Low Leadership Signals: lead one improvement or mentor one colleague.
- Low Strategic Thinking: link work to business priorities in updates.
- Low Influence: prepare stakeholder-specific communication.

## Leadership Readiness Assessment

Purpose: measure whether the user is developing behaviors required to lead people and outcomes.

### Components

| Component | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Feedback | 20% |
| Coaching | 15% |
| Delegation | 15% |
| Accountability | 15% |
| Communication | 15% |
| Trust | 20% |

### Questions

Feedback:

1. I can give specific feedback without avoiding the real issue.
2. I give positive and corrective feedback close to the moment.
3. I can separate behavior, impact, and expectation.
4. I am comfortable receiving feedback without becoming defensive.

Coaching:

5. I ask questions before giving advice.
6. I help others think through problems instead of solving everything for them.
7. I adapt my support based on the person's skill and confidence.
8. I follow up on development conversations.

Delegation:

9. I know what work I should stop owning directly.
10. I set clear outcomes, owners, and deadlines.
11. I delegate with context instead of dumping tasks.
12. I allow others to learn without taking back ownership too quickly.

Accountability:

13. I set clear expectations.
14. I address missed commitments directly.
15. I distinguish between skill gaps, will gaps, and unclear expectations.
16. I follow through consistently.

Communication:

17. I communicate decisions clearly.
18. I make next steps and ownership explicit.
19. I adjust communication for audience and context.
20. I reduce confusion during change or uncertainty.

Trust:

21. People can rely on me to do what I say.
22. I listen before judging.
23. I handle sensitive information responsibly.
24. I create space for people to raise concerns.

### Progression Levels

| Score | Level |
|---:|---|
| 0-39 | Individual Contributor Mindset |
| 40-59 | Informal Leader |
| 60-74 | Emerging Manager |
| 75-89 | Reliable People Leader |
| 90-100 | Strategic Leader |

## New Manager Readiness Assessment

Purpose: assess readiness for first-time managers, team leads, and emerging leaders.

### Components

| Component | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Role Transition | 15% |
| Delegation | 15% |
| Difficult Conversations | 15% |
| Team Management | 20% |
| Communication | 15% |
| Prioritization | 20% |

### Questions

Role Transition:

1. I understand that management requires different work, not just more work.
2. I am ready to measure success through team outcomes.
3. I can stop being the default problem-solver for every issue.
4. I understand what my team will need from me in the first 90 days.

Delegation:

5. I can identify which work should be delegated.
6. I can explain expectations clearly.
7. I can inspect progress without micromanaging.
8. I can support learning without taking over.

Difficult Conversations:

9. I can address underperformance directly.
10. I can discuss missed expectations without personalizing the issue.
11. I can handle disagreement constructively.
12. I can prepare for emotionally charged conversations.

Team Management:

13. I know how to structure effective 1:1s.
14. I know how to create team operating norms.
15. I can identify early trust or morale issues.
16. I can help team members understand priorities.

Communication:

17. I communicate changes with clarity and context.
18. I can explain decisions even when people disagree.
19. I create alignment around owners and next steps.
20. I know when to escalate issues.

Prioritization:

21. I can distinguish urgent work from important work.
22. I can protect team focus.
23. I can make tradeoffs with incomplete information.
24. I can connect team priorities to broader business goals.

### Score Bands

| Score | Band |
|---:|---|
| 0-39 | Needs Foundation |
| 40-59 | Emerging But Inconsistent |
| 60-74 | Ready With Support |
| 75-89 | Strong New Manager Candidate |
| 90-100 | High Readiness |

## Scoring Engine Output

Each assessment returns:

- Overall score.
- Component scores.
- Top 3 strengths.
- Top 3 blockers.
- 3 immediate actions.
- 90-day recommended focus.
- Human escalation trigger.

## Sample User Report

User: Promotion-focused senior individual contributor.

Career Health Score: 68 - Stable.

Promotion Readiness Score: 61 - Build Evidence First.

Strengths:

- Strong results in current role.
- Good manager relationship.
- Clear desire for next level.

Blockers:

- Low visibility outside immediate team.
- Promotion evidence is scattered.
- Strategic thinking is not showing up in updates.

Next 7 Days:

1. Create a promotion evidence inventory with 5 measurable wins.
2. Ask manager what evidence would make a promotion case credible.
3. Identify 3 stakeholders who should understand current project impact.

90-Day Focus:

Build a credible promotion case by strengthening evidence, visibility, and stakeholder support before asking for title or compensation change.

Human Escalation Trigger:

Book a promotion review after evidence inventory and manager expectations are complete.
