# Career Lab HQ - Version 2 Validation Strategy

## Strategic Pivot

Career Lab HQ should not build a large coaching platform first. The first product should validate whether people will pay for a focused Career Operating System that creates measurable progress.

Active Phase 1 scope:

- Career Assessment
- Career Health Score
- Promotion Readiness Score
- Leadership Readiness Score
- New Manager Readiness Score
- Career Blueprint
- Weekly Check-In System
- AI Career Advisor
- Human Coaching Escalation

Out of scope for Phase 1: community, courses, certifications, enterprise, complex admin, advanced resource library, corporate programs, and coaching marketplaces.

## 1. Why Pay Instead Of Using ChatGPT?

ChatGPT gives advice. Career Lab HQ must provide a structured operating system.

Users pay for Career Lab HQ if it does four things better than ChatGPT:

1. Diagnoses their career situation with a consistent framework.
2. Scores readiness and progress over time.
3. Converts advice into a 90-day action plan.
4. Tells them exactly what to do next every week.

The promise should be:

You do not need more career advice. You need a system that shows where you stand, what is blocking progress, and what to do next.

## 2. Proprietary Frameworks

The defensible product is the scoring and operating model, not the AI chat.

Core proprietary frameworks:

- Career Health Score
- Promotion Readiness Score
- Leadership Readiness Score
- New Manager Readiness Score
- 90-Day Career Blueprint
- Weekly Career Operating Review
- Human Escalation Triggers

These frameworks create differentiation because they turn subjective career anxiety into measurable categories, progress trends, and next actions.

## 3. Smallest Product That Creates Outcomes

The smallest viable Career Operating System:

1. Assessment: 15-25 questions across clarity, role alignment, promotion readiness, leadership behaviors, interview readiness, and action consistency.
2. Scorecard: Career Health Score plus one primary readiness score.
3. 90-Day Blueprint: target outcome, milestones, weekly priorities, risks, and next actions.
4. Weekly Check-In: completed actions, blockers, confidence, new context, score updates.
5. AI Advisor: explains the score, rewrites actions, prepares conversations, and answers tactical questions using the user's blueprint.
6. Human Escalation: paid review when the user has a high-stakes event or low score plateau.

This can be built and validated without courses, community, complex admin, or a large content library.

## 4. First Three Paid Features

Feature 1: Career Scorecard

The user pays because they finally see where they stand. The scorecard should feel more valuable than generic advice because it identifies constraints: low visibility, unclear goal, weak evidence, poor interview readiness, or inconsistent action.

Feature 2: 90-Day Career Blueprint

The user pays because the platform converts a messy career problem into a concrete plan. The plan must include weekly actions and visible milestones.

Feature 3: Weekly Next-Best Action

The user pays because every login answers: what should I do next? This is the retention loop.

## 5. 30-Day Launchable MVP

Day 1-7:

- Finalize assessment questions.
- Define scoring formulas.
- Create manual or semi-automated score report.
- Create a simple landing page with one paid beta offer.

Day 8-14:

- Build intake form.
- Generate scorecard and 90-day blueprint.
- Create weekly check-in form.
- Set up Stripe payment link.

Day 15-21:

- Recruit 10-25 beta users.
- Run onboarding calls manually.
- Deliver first reports.
- Track willingness to pay, completion, and perceived value.

Day 22-30:

- Run first weekly check-ins.
- Compare score movement and action completion.
- Interview users.
- Refine offer, pricing, and frameworks.

No full portal is required for the first validation cycle. A lightweight web app, form workflow, or even high-touch concierge MVP is enough.

## 6. Validation With Fewer Than 25 Beta Users

Beta offer:

Career Lab HQ 90-Day Career Operating System Beta.

Price test:

- Option A: $49 for diagnostic and 90-day blueprint.
- Option B: $99 for diagnostic, blueprint, and 4 weekly check-ins.
- Option C: $299 for diagnostic, blueprint, 4 check-ins, and one human review.

Validation criteria:

- At least 40% of qualified users pay something.
- At least 70% say the scorecard revealed something useful.
- At least 60% complete the first weekly check-in.
- At least 30% request human review or ask about continuing.
- Users can clearly explain why this is different from ChatGPT.

If users will not pay $49-$99 for the diagnostic and plan, do not build the larger platform.

## 7. Fastest Path To First Paying Customer

Sell a focused outcome, not a platform.

Best first offer:

Promotion Readiness Diagnostic + 90-Day Action Plan.

Price:

$99-$299 depending on whether human review is included.

Why this offer:

- Promotion has economic upside.
- Users understand the value.
- The assessment and scoring model are highly relevant.
- It creates natural follow-up: weekly check-ins, manager conversation prep, evidence bank, and coaching review.

Secondary offer:

New Manager Readiness Diagnostic + First 90 Days Plan.

This may become the strongest long-term wedge because new managers have urgent pain and employers may eventually sponsor it.

## Career Health Score

Purpose:

Measure whether a user's career is moving in a clear, intentional, and sustainable direction.

Components and weighting:

- Career Clarity: 20%
- Role Alignment: 15%
- Readiness For Next Move: 20%
- Skill Development: 15%
- Relationship And Influence Health: 15%
- Action Consistency: 15%

Inputs:

- Target role clarity
- Desired salary or responsibility level
- Current role satisfaction
- Skill gaps
- Recent accomplishments
- Manager alignment
- Networking and stakeholder activity
- Weekly action completion
- Confidence level

Scoring logic:

Each component is scored 0-100. Weighted total produces the Career Health Score.

Score bands:

- 0-39: At Risk
- 40-59: Unclear
- 60-74: Stable
- 75-89: Building Momentum
- 90-100: High Momentum

Recommendations:

- Low clarity: define target role and decision criteria.
- Low alignment: adjust target path or identify transition plan.
- Low readiness: build evidence and close skill gaps.
- Low influence: increase visibility and stakeholder relationships.
- Low consistency: reduce plan size and complete one weekly action.

## Promotion Readiness Score

Purpose:

Measure whether the user has enough evidence, visibility, relationships, and strategic contribution to make a credible promotion case.

Components and weighting:

- Results: 25%
- Visibility: 20%
- Relationships: 15%
- Leadership Signals: 15%
- Strategic Thinking: 15%
- Influence: 10%

Inputs:

- Quantified wins
- Scope of responsibility
- Manager feedback
- Cross-functional exposure
- Stakeholder trust
- Examples of leading without authority
- Business impact
- Promotion timeline
- Current level expectations

Scoring logic:

Each category is scored 0-5, then normalized to 100 and weighted.

Improvement actions:

- Results: document measurable wins and business impact.
- Visibility: create a visibility plan and share progress in the right forums.
- Relationships: map decision-makers and influencers.
- Leadership signals: mentor others, lead initiatives, improve judgment.
- Strategic thinking: connect work to business priorities.
- Influence: practice stakeholder alignment and persuasive communication.

Promotion readiness bands:

- 0-49: Not ready to ask.
- 50-69: Build evidence first.
- 70-84: Prepare promotion case.
- 85-100: Ready for promotion conversation.

## Leadership Readiness Score

Purpose:

Measure whether the user is developing the behaviors required to lead people and outcomes.

Components and weighting:

- Feedback Skills: 20%
- Coaching Ability: 15%
- Delegation: 15%
- Trust Building: 20%
- Accountability: 15%
- Communication: 15%

Inputs:

- Comfort giving feedback
- Frequency of coaching conversations
- Delegation patterns
- Team trust indicators
- Handling missed expectations
- Meeting and communication habits
- Conflict response
- Decision clarity

Progression levels:

- Level 1: Individual Contributor Mindset
- Level 2: Informal Leader
- Level 3: Emerging Manager
- Level 4: Reliable People Leader
- Level 5: Strategic Leader

Recommendations:

- Low feedback: use a feedback script and practice one low-stakes conversation.
- Low coaching: replace advice-giving with better questions.
- Low delegation: identify one task to transfer with clear expectations.
- Low trust: improve follow-through and 1:1 consistency.
- Low accountability: define standards and consequences.
- Low communication: clarify decisions, owners, and next steps.

## New Manager Readiness Score

Purpose:

Assess readiness for first-time managers, team leads, and emerging leaders.

Components and weighting:

- Role Transition Awareness: 15%
- Delegation Readiness: 15%
- Feedback And Coaching: 20%
- Team Operating Rhythm: 15%
- Difficult Conversations: 15%
- Prioritization And Decision-Making: 10%
- Manager Communication: 10%

Inputs:

- Experience leading projects or people
- Comfort setting expectations
- Ability to stop doing everything personally
- 1:1 readiness
- Feedback confidence
- Conflict comfort
- Planning habits
- Understanding of manager responsibilities

Score bands:

- 0-39: Needs foundation.
- 40-59: Emerging but inconsistent.
- 60-74: Ready with support.
- 75-89: Strong new manager candidate.
- 90-100: High readiness.

Core recommendations:

- Build first 30/60/90 manager plan.
- Create 1:1 structure.
- Practice feedback and expectation-setting.
- Define delegation map.
- Identify first team trust risks.

This score is a strong candidate for the first monetized product line.

## MVP User Journey

Starting state:

The user feels stuck and does not know what to do next.

Step 1: Assessment

The user answers targeted questions. They choose a primary goal: promotion, leadership growth, interview readiness, or new manager readiness.

Step 2: Scorecard

The user receives Career Health Score plus one readiness score. The report explains what is helping, what is blocking progress, and what matters most now.

Step 3: 90-Day Blueprint

The system creates:

- Target outcome
- 3 milestones
- Weekly focus areas
- Top risks
- Specific next actions
- Human escalation trigger

Step 4: Weekly Check-In

The user reports what was completed, what changed, what is blocked, and how confident they feel.

Step 5: Next-Best Action

The system updates the plan and assigns the next action.

Ending state:

The user has a clear plan, measurable progress, and knows exactly what to do next.

## Recommended Phase 1 Pricing

Do not start with a low-value $19 subscription.

Test paid outcomes first:

- $49: Career Diagnostic and Scorecard
- $99: Diagnostic, Scorecard, and 90-Day Blueprint
- $299: Blueprint plus one human review
- $499: 30-day Promotion or New Manager Sprint

After validation, convert to subscription:

- $39-$59/month: operating system, weekly check-ins, score tracking, AI advisor
- $149-$249/month: includes monthly human review or specialist credit

## What I Would Build First With My Own Money

I would not build the full platform yet.

I would build a concierge MVP around Promotion Readiness and New Manager Readiness:

1. Landing page with one clear offer.
2. Assessment form.
3. Scorecard generator.
4. 90-day blueprint output.
5. Weekly check-in workflow.
6. Stripe payment link.

This validates the business before investing in a full SaaS product.

## Decision Gates

Proceed only if:

- Users pay for the diagnostic and blueprint.
- Users complete weekly check-ins.
- Users can describe the value without saying "AI chatbot."
- Users ask for continued tracking or human review.
- The founder can repeatedly sell the same outcome.

Kill or reposition if:

- Users treat it as a one-time report.
- Users compare it directly to ChatGPT and do not see the difference.
- Weekly check-ins are ignored.
- The strongest interest is only resume editing.

## Phase 1 North Star

The North Star is not chat volume, resource views, or page visits.

The North Star is:

Weekly completed career actions per active member.

Supporting metrics:

- Assessment completion rate
- Paid diagnostic conversion
- Blueprint completion rate
- Week 1 check-in completion
- Week 4 retention
- Score improvement
- Human review conversion
